OUR LEGACY

A 1,000 Year History of Khmer Weaving

From the golden age of Angkor to the modern revival, Khmer silk weaving is an art form passed through generations of master artisans. Each pattern tells a story of royalty, divinity, and the resilient spirit of Cambodia.

Our atelier collaborates with rural weaving communities in Takéo and Siem Reap to preserve the rare 'Hol' (Ikat) and 'Pidan' (Pictorial) techniques, ensuring this heritage breathes in the contemporary world.

The Hands Behind the Gold

01.

The Sourcing

We only use 100% natural mulberry silk from Cambodian silk farms, ensuring a textile that is as ethical as it is beautiful.

02.

The Dyeing

Our master dyers use ancient natural pigments—indigo, ebony bark, and lac insects—to achieve deep, organic colors.

03.

The Finish

Each garment is hand-finished in our Phnom Penh atelier with meticulous attention to tailoring and structural integrity.
